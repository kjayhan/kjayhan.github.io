Self-studying statistics

Statistical Rethinking

A great book and even greater lectures by a top scholar, Richard McElreath. (He is recently uploading the 2023 version of the lectures here.

There is a tidyverse translation of the book here by A. Solomon Kurz.

All the better, “rethinking” involves Bayesian approach to inference, rather than a frequentist one.

Regression and Other Stories

A great book by three legends of Bayesian Data Analysis: Regression and Other Stories by Andrew Gelman, Jennifer Hill, and Aki Vehtari. You can find free online pdf of the book, the code and data for the examples and more from the links.

Once you are done with this book, the next stage could be Bayesian Data Analysis by Andrew Gelman, John Carlin, Hal Stern, David Dunson, Aki Vehtari, and Donald Rubin. This book’s pdf is also freely available on their website.

Aki Vehtari also provide freely available course using this book. However, both BDA and Aki’s lectures are more advanced than Statistical Rethinking, and Regression and Other Stories.

The Effect: An Introduction to Research Design and Causality

This book by Nick Huntington-Klein is more student-friendly I should say. It is a great introduction to econometrics with code examples in R, Stata and Python

You can find the course lectures here.
